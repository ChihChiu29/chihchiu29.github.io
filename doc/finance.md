A very compact way to remember everything is:

Action | Stock | Call | Put
Buy | Long Stock | Long Call | Long Put
Sell | Exit Long Stock (or Short Stock if opening) | Short Call | Short Put

For options specifically:

Long = bought the contract (rights)
Short = sold the contract (obligations)


The easiest way to remember options is:
Buy = rights
Sell = obligations
And:
Call = buy stock
Put = sell stock


Bullish: Buy Call, Sell Put
Bearish: Buy Put, Sell Call
Limited risk: Buy Call, Buy Put
Potentially large risk: Sell Call, Sell Put

For options: Long = bought, Short = sold


Professional shorthand
Traders often describe the resulting position instead of the order:
Long Put	Bought a put and still own it
Short Put	Sold a put and still owe the obligation
Long Call	Bought a call and still own it
Short Call	Sold a call and still owe the obligation
